The Touch of Music: Guitarist's Journey

Exploring the tactile and emotional connection of a guitarist with her instrument.

Introduction: Self-taught guitarist since secondary school, she views her guitar as a ‘best friend,’ a companion that supports her on her musical journey.

Fingerpicking and Percussive Rhythms

"At this moment, it felt more dynamic, because I was doing fingerpicking and some percussive movements, so it felt like I had a more rhythmic feel of the guitar rather than if I were just strumming."

This video showcases her fingerpicking and percussive movements, highlighting her dynamic connection with the guitar.

Background & Origin

Starting in secondary school, she was drawn to the guitar by her school’s strong music culture. Friends taught her basic chords, and she was soon captivated by the guitar's versatility for accompaniment and its ease of learning compared to the piano.

Comfort & Physical Adaptation

Initially, pressing on steel strings caused discomfort and limited her playing time. Over time, callouses developed, allowing her to play comfortably and adopt techniques to minimize strain, highlighting the physical adaptation required to master her instrument.

Tactile Awareness & Rhythmic Connection

Playing guitar requires a unique tactile awareness, especially during fingerpicking and rhythmic tapping, as seen in the video. She describes this physical engagement as a meaningful part of her relationship with her instrument, contrasting the steadiness of strumming.

Emotional Bond with the Guitar

She views her guitar as a “best friend” rather than an extension of herself. Her attachment goes beyond functionality; she meticulously cleans and cares for her guitar, showing it the care she would for a close companion.

Mastery of Touch & Identity Development

As her guitar skills improve, she increasingly sees herself as a musician. She believes that achieving greater touch mastery will ultimately affirm her identity as a guitarist, highlighting the importance of technical control in building a musical identity.
